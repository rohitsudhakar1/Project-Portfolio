03 About

I'm a senior at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, double-majoring in Computer Science and Computer Engineering. I like work that crosses layers — a model that's no good unless it ships, firmware that's no good unless it holds a deadline, a UI that's no good unless someone uses it twice.

Most recently I built XNS, an ML categorization platform that processed 1.4M+ banking records, at Ardent Capital. Before that I was on the Wisconsin Autonomous Club's perception team. In my own time I keep one embedded project and one full-stack app moving in parallel — current rotation is the FreeRTOS work and patch-resume.

Outside engineering: tennis (NTRP 4.0-ish, still climbing), film photography, and an ongoing argument with myself about whether to learn Rust before I graduate.
